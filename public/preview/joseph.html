Turan & Ebz

A husband, a wife, and a lot of fish.

Turan was born and bred in Southwark and has spent most of his life within a mile of this shop. Ebz is from Hackney, just over the river, which he has learned not to bring up.

Ebz fell for Turan's chiselled jawline and ocean brown eyes. Turan couldn't help but fall for her bouncy personality. Fast forward a few years and they now live together and work together, which means they never get a break from one another.

They still seem to like each other. Most days.

We opened for the neighbourhood first — sourcing British, keeping it clean, and knowing your order before you've said it. Whether you're in every Friday or you've never been, you're part of it.

The name

Joseph

The shop is named after Turan's father.

He isn't here to see it. But the way we buy the fish, the way we treat people who walk through the door, and the fact that we'd rather close early than serve you something we wouldn't eat ourselves — all of that came from him.

It's his name above the door for a reason.

The fish

We're fussy about three things.

Everything else about a chippy is negotiable. These aren't.

Fresh

Never frozen. It comes in, it gets cut, it gets fried. If we run out of something, we've run out — we won't defrost a substitute and hope you don't notice.

British

Bought from British boats and British growers. Not because it's a nice line for a website, but because it's fresher and it keeps someone here in work.

Sustainable

We won't sell you a fish that's being emptied out of the sea. Sometimes that means the menu changes. That's the trade, and we're fine with it.
