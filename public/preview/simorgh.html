Why Simorgh

Thirty birds went looking for a god. They found each other.

In the old Persian poem, thirty birds cross the world in search of the Simorgh — a great bird said to have the power to heal. When they finally arrive, they find no god waiting. They find only themselves. Si morgh, in Farsi, means thirty birds.

She has been our family's emblem for as long as we've been cooking. And a bird known for healing seemed like the right thing to hang above an old pharmacy.

All of us as one, and one of us as all.
The kitchen

Charcoal, saffron, and a lot of bread.

Kebabs over live coals, casseroles that take all morning, and nan baked to order — because Persian food without hot bread isn't really Persian food.
